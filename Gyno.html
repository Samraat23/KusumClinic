Gynaecology

Gynaecology focuses on the well-being of the women reproductive system. This includes the uterus, ovaries, fallopian tubes, and breasts. Gynaecologists are women specialists who are involved in routine check-ups and treatment of female reproductive issues.

A Gynaecological Visit

It comprises of discussion and consultation on various women health aspects. It also includes General Health Assessment: Discussing overall health, including weight, blood pressure, and other vital signs. A gynaecologist performs an internal Examination to look for the external and internal reproductive organs. It may include a Pap smear to screen for cervical cancer. Breast Examination is done to check for lumps or other changes in the breast tissue.

What are the Common Gynaecological Conditions

Menstrual Disorders: Irregular periods, heavy bleeding, PMS, PMDD.


Reproductive Health Issues:PCOS, endometriosis, uterine fibroids, ovarian cysts.


Infections:Yeast infections, bacterial vaginosis, sexually transmitted infections (STIs).


Cancers:Cervical, ovarian, uterine, and breast cancers


Questions to Ask Your Gynaecologist

Menstrual Health: What is considered a normal menstrual cycle? What symptoms should I be concerned about?


Contraception:It is the most neglected aspect of women health and is never dealt in detail. What birth control options are available and what are their side effects?


Screenings:How often should I get a Pap smear, HPV test, and mammogram?


Fertility:What are the various methods which can help in conceiving?


Menopause:What are the signs of menopause and how can I manage symptoms?


Sexual Health:Majority of women are unaware of sexual health wellness and rarely consult a doctor for the same.


Infections:How can I prevent and treat common vaginal infections and STIs?
