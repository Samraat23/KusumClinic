Vaginal Infections

Vaginal infections are common conditions that affects women across all ages. They occur when there is an discrepancy in the normal vaginal flora which allows harmful bacteria, fungus or other organisms to overgrow. Here are some common types of vaginal infections



What are the types vaginal infections:Different types of vaginal infections, such as fungal infections and bacterial vaginitis have different symptoms and treatments. Understanding the specific type of infection is crucial for effective treatment.



What are the possible causes of vaginal infection:Vaginal infections can be caused by various factors, including bacteria, yeast, parasites, hormonal changes, or sexually transmitted infections (STIs). Sexual active people have more of these infections.



What are the symptoms of vaginal infection:Symptoms can include itching outside or inside both, burning, fishy smelling discharge, pain during urination or intercourse, and redness or swelling of the vaginal area.



How is this infection diagnosed:Gynaecologist perform a general check-up, internal examination and laboratory tests, such as a vaginal culture or swab test to diagnose the infection accurately.



What are the treatment options:Treatment may include medications, such as antifungal creams, antibiotics, or antiparasitic medications. A gynaecologist can recommend the most appropriate treatment based on the symptoms and examination of infection.



Is it to possible to prevent future infections:Practicing good hygiene, avoiding irritants like scented products, wearing breathable cotton underwear, and practicing safe sex are some of the tips that can really help to prevent recurrence.



Is Partner testing also needed:Depending on the type of infection, partner may be tested or treated to prevent reinfection or transmission.
