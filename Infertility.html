Infertility

Infertility is defined as the inability to conceive or achieve a successful pregnancy after one year of regular, unprotected intercourse for women under the age of 35, or after six months for women over the age of 35. Infertility can affect both men and women and may be due to various factors.



What are the causes for infertility : Infertility can be due to many reasons like ovulation disorders (PCOS), Tubal blockage, uterine factors or male semen abnormalities. Understanding the underlying causes of infertility can help guide further evaluation and treatment.



What tests are required to diagnose the cause of infertility : Various tests such as hormone tests, ultrasound imaging, semen analysis, and tubal evaluation, HSG , diagnostic laparoscopy or hysteroscopy can help to identify factors contributing to infertility.



What are the treatment options for infertility : Medications, intrauterine insemination (IUI), in vitro fertilization (IVF), surgical procedures, and assisted reproductive technologies (ART) are the various options available depending upon the cause of infertility



What are the success rates of different infertility treatments : It largely depends upon the cause of infertility. PCOS can be tackled with medicines alone while tubal blockage needs surgery to open up the tube and usually have good results.



What are the potential risks and side effects of infertility treatments : Understanding the potential risks and side effects associated with infertility treatments can help you make informed decisions about your care.



What can be done to increase chances of conception naturally : Pregnancy is a journey of learning and gynecologists offer guidance and support to pregnant women on various topics, including nutrition, exercise, childbirth preparation, breastfeeding, and postpartum care. They address any questions or concerns the mother may have and provide education about pregnancy, childbirth, and newborn care.



When to consult a fertility specialist : Consulting a fertility specialist early is advisable rather than late. Most often people delay in seeking advice and waste the most crucial months or years of their reproductive life. . Open and detailed communication with your gynaecologist is essential for navigating the complex issues of infertility and having a personalized treatment programme.
